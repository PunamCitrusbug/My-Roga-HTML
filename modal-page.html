How To Choose a Vacuum Cleaner For Carpet?

There are many different types of vacuums on the market, making a vacuum purchase confusing and overwhelming. The following is an overview of vacuum types and some considerations when choosing the best carpet vacuum to maintain carpet quality and life.

The main considerations when choosing a vacuum cleaner are the size of the cleaning area (house, office, garage etc), the vacuum type (stick, canister, robotic etc) which determines its portability, its vacuuming power, its suitability to clean the required surfaces, and lastly if it should be corded or cordless.

To learn more about vacuum cleaners for carpets, continue reading below to discover additional information about the topic.

Which cordless vacuum is best for carpets?

Though upright vacuum cleaners may do the best job in vacuuming carpets, most recommended models are wired in order to give a high suction power. In case you can compromise on power, and prefer the convenience of a cordless vacuum cleaner, there are great cordless vacuums that can deliver a good performance on carpets as well as on other surfaces.

Upright vacuum cleaners tend to be the best option for larger, carpeted households. This is because they usually feature a turbo brush in the floor head, which makes them particularly effective at picking up hair and residue from carpets.

These models are more stable and easier to manoeuvre than cylinders, as you’re pushing them forward rather than dragging them behind you. There’s also no need to bend when using an upright, which makes them a good option for those with back issues.
